WHY LA ROSI\xc3\x88RE IN TARENTAISE VALLEY? \nFamily friendly resort with a \'Famille Plus\' labelAttractive and sunny resortGreat beginner\'s areaInternational skiing (Italy) \n\n\xc2\xa0\nLa Rosi\xc3\xa8re ski resort is linked with La Thuile in Italy and is the only Savoyard resort to offer an international ski area: 160km of marked pisted runs (Espace San Bernardo). In terms of location, the resort has a lot to offer. It is built beside the road that zigzags its way up from Bourg-St-Maurice to the Petit-St-Bernard pass to Italy. The ski resort and ski slopes are south facing, attracting a lot of sunshine and have great views of the Vall\xc3\xa9es of Les Arcs and La Plagne. It is also close to the, Vanoise National Park \xe2\x80\x93 the first to open in France. \n\n\xc2\xa0\nLa Rosi\xc3\xa8re has controlled development, so buildings are constructed using traditional methods and are in keeping with local taste.Recognised for its high level of family-friendliness La Rosi\xc3\xa8re has been awarded the highest official \xe2\x80\x98Famille Plus Montagne\xe2\x80\x99 award.The ski resort of La Rosi\xc3\xa8re was created on 23rd December 1960 with 1 ski lift, 1 ski lift attendant, 1 hotel with a restaurant/bar and 1 ski instructor! It became an international ski resort in 1985 when the new Italian link to La Thuile was opened. Since then it has been transformed into a first class ski resort without letting go of its traditions or village atmosphere, and is one of Ski Collection\xe2\x80\x99s most popular ski resorts.Every Sunday the Tourist office of La Rosi\xc3\xa8re will welcome you to the ice bar especially created in the shape of the number 50, where you can enjoy hot mulled wine or a hot chocolate and find out the week\xe2\x80\x99s activities and events within the ski resort.There are two main resort centres, the original centre and Les Eucherts village, each with their own amenities including shops, a high-speed chairlift and ski lesson meeting points. The two areas are conveniently linked via a pathway through the forest.
