followers).</p>\n<p>There are key differences between using Twitter via short or long code:</p>\n<h3>Using a short code</h3>\n<p>Using Twitter by texting to a short code means you can perform actions and access content like you would via the web or a smart device.</p>\n<p>When you use a short code with your Twitter account, you can post Tweets, receive notifications, and enroll in security features like <a href="/articles/20170388">login verification</a>.</p>\n<p>We currently support two-way Tweets (sending and receiving) via text message through short codes provided by our supported code</a>.</li>\n<li>Text the word <strong>START</strong> or <strong>SIGNUP</strong> to your mobile carrier\xe2\x80\x99s short code.</li>\n<li>Use these designated <a href="/articles/14020">Twitter commands</a> to perform actions from your Twitter account</li>\n</ol>\n<h3>Using a long code</h3>\n<p>If your country and carrier are not listed as having a short code, it may be possible for you to post a Tweet using any of our Twitter long codes.</p>\n<p>Long codes listed below are one-way only. You can send Tweets that will post to your profile (and be sent to your followers), but you will not be able to receive Tweets or other Twitter notifications to your phone through this channel.</p>\n<p>Note: For these reasons, we do not recommend features such as login verification for long code users, as the experience may be less than optimal.</p>\n<p>Subscribers do not have to be
