class="ocpArticleContent" id="ocpArticleContent">\n<!--Last Published: 2/12/2016 7:58:35 AM PST-->\n<h1>What\'s new in Outlook 2016 for Windows</h1>\n<section class="ocpIntroduction">\n<p>Outlook 2016 for Windows has all the tools and functionality you\'ve used before, as well as new and improved features for professional email, calendar, contacts, and task management  to help you communicate faster and easier. \r\n\r\n</p>\n<p>Here are some of the top new and improved features you\'ll find in \r\nOutlook 2016:</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>Email attachments</h2>\n<p>With Outlook 2016, you can save time by easily attaching documents that you most recently used to your emails from the Ribbon.   When you attach files from OneDrive, OneDrive for Business or SharePoint, you can share them as View only, or give the recipients Edit permissions, which helps them collaborate easily on one copy that everyone works on. </p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Attach a file in Outlook 2016" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/9833dedf-523b-4ccf-be02-cfacefe16f1d.png" title="Attach a file in Outlook 2016"/>\n</p>\n<p>For more information, see <a class="ocpArticleLink" href="Attach-a-file-to-an-email-in-Outlook-2016-bdfafef5-792a-42b1-9a7b-84512d7de7fc" title="Attach a file to an email in Outlook 2016">Attach a file to an email in Outlook 2016</a>.</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>Outlook 2016 Groups (Office 365 work or school account)</h2>\n<p>With Outlook 2016 you can use Groups instead of distribution lists to communicate and collaborate with your team members. </p>\n<p>Groups are the better way to collaborate because: </p>\n<ul>\n<li>\n<p>You can create groups with a few clicks and organize them around topics and conversations to work with your teams.</p>\n</li>\n<li>\n<p>With Groups in Outlook 2016, teams now have a shared place for all their email conversations with full history, allowing teams to engage with members right from the Group\xe2\x80\x99s inbox, create team events, join existing Groups or create new ones.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Groups on the left nav bar" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/ba6c51d2-a3d1-4ce0-ab0c-5c3a581e6fca.png" title="Groups on the left nav bar"/>\n</p>\n</li>\n<li>\n<p>You can add a group as a favorite just like a folder. Adding a group as a favorite gives you full access to conversation history when you are offline. </p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Groups in Favorites" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/b99de4e0-2f03-454e-bc77-c608a4e5ccda.png" title="Groups in Favorites"/>\n</p>\n</li>\n<li>\n<p>Get access to past conversations and history for Groups you have joined or added as a member.</p>\n</li>\n<li>\n<p>Within the Groups shared space, messages are grouped together for each conversation to facilitate quick communication with your team.  You can "Like" a message within a conversation and post a quick reply to have a quick back and forth conversations with your team. </p>\n<p>You can also receive social notifications in Outlook when someone "Likes" your message. </p>\n</li>\n<li>\n<p>Schedule meetings on a group calendar that everyone in group can update.</p>\n</li>\n<li>\n<p>You can access a group\xe2\x80\x99s conversation in your Inbox by subscribing to a group. This makes it behave like a distribution list. You can also unsubscribe at any time. </p>\n</li>\n<li>\n<p>Manage groups from Outlook by adding or removing members. </p>\n</li>\n</ul>\n<p>For information about how to create a Group, see <a class="ocpArticleLink" href="Create-a-group-in-Outlook-2016-04d0c9cf-6864-423c-a380-4fa858f27102" title="Create a group in Outlook 2016">Create a group in Outlook 2016</a>. Make sure to check the topics at the bottom of that article to learn about joining and managing groups, as well as other features.</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>Search</h2>\n<p>Outlook 2016 email search is faster, more reliable and provides improved performance. You can seamlessly search email across your local computer, Exchange 16 server or Office 365 Mailbox.</p>\n<p>Outlook makes it easier to find email by offering keyword and people suggestions based on your mailbox content from Exchange 2016.  You can quickly find email from someone that you\'ve been exchanging emails with or a project you\xe2\x80\x99re working on. Simply start typing someone\'s name or a keyword in the search box to see suggestions from Exchange 2016.</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>Use Clutter to sort low priority messages</h2>\n<p>Clutter helps you move low priority messages out of your Inbox so that you can easily scan for important messages. Clutter analyzes your emails, and based on your past behavior, determines the messages that you\xe2\x80\x99re most likely to ignore. It then automatically moves those messages to a folder called <b class="ocpUI">Clutter</b> so that you can review them later.</p>\n<p>You can even help Clutter learn your preferences faster by manually moving items in or out of the Clutter folder.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Move to Clutter" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/b11c594a-0363-4fb9-9d4e-c080d61647fd.png" title="Move to Clutter"/>\n</p>\n<p>\n<b class="ocpNote">\n<include item="note"></include> Note </b>\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0If you bought Office 2016 for Windows as a one-time purchase, and you don\xe2\x80\x99t have an Office 365 subscription, you won\xe2\x80\x99t have access to some features, such as Clutter. Office 365 doesn\xe2\x80\x99t support creation of international address at the time of the release of Outlook 2016. However, if you\'re an Office 365 user, you can send mail to and receive email from any international address. We recommend that you use Unicode Outlook data files (pst and ost files) when using EAI. </p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>Tell Me</h2>\n<p>Tell Me brings features and help topics  to your fingerprints, and saves you the time you would normally use to look for a specific function on the Ribbon.
