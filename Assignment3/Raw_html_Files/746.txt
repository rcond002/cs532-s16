class="ocpIntroduction">\n<p>PowerPoint 2016 for Windows has all the functionality and features you\'re used to with some added enhancements and the best new features from Office 2016. </p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>January 2016</h2>\n<p>\n<b class="ocpNote">\n<include item="note"></include> Note </b>\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The following features are only available to <a class="ocpExternalLink" href="https://products.office.com/en-us/buy/compare-microsoft-office-products" target="_blank" title="Office 365 subscribers">Office 365 subscribers</a>. They will first roll out to <a class="ocpExternalLink" href="https://office.com/insider" target="_blank" title="Office Insider">Office Insider</a> participants, and later to consumer and commercial Office 365 subscribers. If you have an Office 365 subscription, <a class="ocpExternalLink" href="https://support.office.com/article/ee68f6cf-422f-464a-82ec-385f65391350" target="_blank" title="make sure you have the latest version of Office">make sure you have the latest version of Office</a>.</p>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Real Time Presence</h3>\n<p>Collaboration is getting better and better in PowerPoint 2016 for Windows. When you share your presentation with others using <a class="ocpArticleLink" href="OneDrive-cdbec569-a18f-4bdc-92e8-d07a8ab5eb95" title="OneDrive">OneDrive</a> or <a class="ocpArticleLink" href="Office-365-SharePoint-4306e7dd-8989-4864-bfaa-a653174ffc35" title="Office 365 SharePoint">Office 365 SharePoint</a>, you can work together in real time. You can now not only see when others are working on the same shared presentation as you, but also see an indicator that shows you exactly what slides they\'re working on, and where in the slides they\'re working. To learn more about real time co-authoring, see <a class="ocpArticleLink" href="Collaborate-on-PowerPoint-presentations-with-real-time-coauthoring-0c30ee3f-8674-4f0e-97be-89cf2892a34d" title="Collaborate on PowerPoint presentations with real time co-authoring">Collaborate on PowerPoint presentations with real time co-authoring</a>. </p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows the icon indicating someone else is collaborating on a slide in PowerPoint 2016" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/df00e0a1-f600-4921-b6f2-2ef359b77888.png" title="Shows the icon indicating someone else is collaborating on a slide in PowerPoint 2016"/>\n</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows icon showing someone working on a section of a slide in PowerPoint 2016 for Windows" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/083d0dcd-3177-43f7-8334-039a0c7e480d.png" title="Shows icon showing someone working on a section of a slide in PowerPoint 2016 for Windows"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Ink Annotation</h3>\n<p>On touch-enabled devices, you\'ll now see a <b class="ocpUI">Draw</b> tab on the ribbon in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint where you can select inking styles and start making ink annotations directly in your files. In PowerPoint, you can also convert your ink drawings to Shapes by selecting them and then selecting <b class="ocpUI">Convert to Shapes</b>. That way, you get the freedom of freeform drawing with the uniformity and standardization of Office graphic shapes. To learn more about inking in Office, see <a class="ocpArticleLink" href="Draw-and-annotate-with-ink-in-Office-2016-c00b7319-2b50-4784-8114-47593d991c5c" title="Draw and annotate with ink in Office 2016">Draw and annotate with ink in Office 2016</a>.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows the Draw tab in PowerPoint 2016 for Windows" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/33e5dfb7-f326-4ad4-8734-f8d19b266c28.png" title="Shows the Draw tab in PowerPoint 2016 for Windows"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Black Theme</h3>\n<p>The highest-contrast Office theme yet has arrived. To change your Office theme, go to <b class="ocpUI">File</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Account</b>, and then click the drop down menu next to <b class="ocpUI">Office Theme</b>. The theme you choose will be applied across all your Office apps. To learn more about Office themes, see <a class="ocpArticleLink" href="Change-the-look-and-feel-of-Office-2016-for-Windows-with-Office-themes-63e65e1c-08d4-4dea-820e-335f54672310" title="Change the look and feel of Office 2016 for Windows with Office themes">Change the look and feel of Office 2016 for Windows with Office themes</a>.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows the Black Theme in PowerPoint 2016 for Windows" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/03d8b796-f1a2-4d6f-9abf-dc48b019f6b2.png" title="Shows the Black Theme in PowerPoint 2016 for Windows"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>November 2015</h2>\n<p>\n<b class="ocpNote">\n<include item="note"></include> Note </b>\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0The following features are only available to <a class="ocpExternalLink" href="https://products.office.com/en-us/buy/compare-microsoft-office-products" target="_blank" title="Office 365 subscribers">Office 365 subscribers</a>. They will first roll out to <a class="ocpExternalLink" href="https://office.com/insider" target="_blank" title="Office Insider">Office Insider</a> participants, and later to consumer and commercial Office 365 subscribers. If you have an Office 365 subscription, <a class="ocpExternalLink" href="https://support.office.com/article/ee68f6cf-422f-464a-82ec-385f65391350" target="_blank" title="make sure you have the latest version of Office">make sure you have the latest version of Office</a>.</p>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Morph Transition</h3>\n<p>PowerPoint 2016 comes with a brand-new transition type, Morph, that can help you make smooth animations, transitions, and object movements across the slides in your presentation. To use the morph transition effectively, you\'ll need to have two slides with at least one object in common\xe2\x80\x94the easiest way is to duplicate the slide and then move the object on the second slide to a different place, or copy and paste the object from one slide and add it to the next. Then, with the second slide selected, go to <b class="ocpUI">Transitions</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Morph</b> to see how morph can automatically animate, move, and emphasize your objects across slides.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows the Morph transition on the Transitions menu in PowerPoint 2016" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/7fff9dd3-3792-4ce7-9f9f-99dacacc10fb.png" title="Shows the Morph transition on the Transitions menu in PowerPoint 2016"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>PowerPoint Designer</h3>\n<p>PowerPoint Designer is a new service that lets you take your content and automatically generate a variety of ideas that you can choose from to make your slides look better. When you add a photo or other unique visual content, the Designer pane will open automatically with a variety of suggested visual treatments you can choose to apply to your slides. Try adding a photo to a title slide to give it a try.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Powerpoint Mobile Powerpoint Designer" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/0b8f4654-f2a2-4246-8142-c7cfa244458e.png" title="Powerpoint Mobile Powerpoint Designer"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h2>September 2015</h2>\n<p>Both Office 365 subscribers and Office 2016 one-time purchasers get the following new features.</p>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Do things quickly with Tell Me</h3>\n<p>\r\n            You\'ll notice a text box on the ribbon in PowerPoint 2016 that says <b class="ocpUI">Tell me what you want to do</b>. This is a text field where you can enter words and phrases related to what you want to do next and quickly get to features you want to use or actions you want to perform. You can also choose to get help related to what you\'re looking for, or perform a Smart Lookup on the term you entered. </p>\n<p>\n<img alt=\'shows the "tell me" search box on the ribbon in PowerPoint\' src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/83ed6352-06eb-44d4-8fd4-48817522c3d7.png" title=\'shows the "tell me" search box on the ribbon in PowerPoint\'/>\n</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows a query being entered into the tell me field on the ribbon in PowerPoint" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/6f7a3727-fc3a-450a-8f37-b983e3bc48bd.png" title="Shows a query being entered into the tell me field on the ribbon in PowerPoint"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Six new chart types</h3>\n<p>Visualizations are critical to effective data analysis as well as compelling storytelling. In PowerPoint 2016, we\'ve added six new charts\xe2\x80\x94with the same rich formatting options that you are familiar with\xe2\x80\x94to help you create some of the most commonly used data visualizations of financial or hierarchal information or for revealing statistical properties in your data.</p>\n<p>When you go to <b class="ocpUI">Insert</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Chart</b> on the ribbon, you\'ll now notice five new options that are especially well-suited for data visualization: <b class="ocpUI">Box and Whisker</b>, <b class="ocpUI">Treemap</b>, <b class="ocpUI">Sunburst</b>, <b class="ocpUI">Histogram</b>, (and within <b class="ocpUI">Histogram</b> options, <b class="ocpUI">Pareto</b>), and <b class="ocpUI">Waterfall</b>.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="shows the new charts in PowerPoint2016" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/d775594a-72d2-4c1e-a807-3a8fd9ed2323.png" title="shows the new charts in PowerPoint2016"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Insights into what you\'re working on</h3>\n<p>The Insights pane, powered by Bing, offers more than just definitions. When you select a word or phrase, right-click it, and choose <b class="ocpUI">Smart Lookup</b>, the insights pane will open with definitions, Wiki articles, and top related searches from the web.  You can also get to Smart Lookup any time by going to <b class="ocpUI">Review</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Smart Lookup</b> and entering a query there.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt=\'Shows the "Smart lookup" option in PowerPoint\' src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/7754a966-1206-4958-b132-f924e7023a73.png" title=\'Shows the "Smart lookup" option in PowerPoint\'/>\n<img alt="Shows Insights Pane in PowerPoint" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/f7597a1b-aac4-4825-a547-b85d67794d60.png" title="Shows Insights Pane in PowerPoint"/>\n</p>\n<p></p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Ink Equations</h3>\n<p>Including math equations has gotten much easier. Now, you can go to <b class="ocpUI">Insert</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Equation</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Ink Equation</b>, any time you want to include a complex math equation in your presentation. If you have a touch device, you can use your finger or a touch stylus to write math equations by hand, and PowerPoint will convert it to text. (If you don\'t have a touch device, you can use a mouse to write, too). You can also erase and select and correct what you\'ve written as you go.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows insert equations button on the ribbon in PowerPoint" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/ec9e4b25-1286-4723-85f6-fdbe8bd61357.png" title="Shows insert equations button on the ribbon in PowerPoint"/>\n</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Show ink equation pane in PowerPoint" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/f3bd02f5-463c-4449-aaf1-d2c5aa382f65.png" title="Show ink equation pane in PowerPoint"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Screen recording</h3>\n<p>Perfect for demonstrations, you can now include screen recordings in your PowerPoint presentations with just a few clicks. Just set up whatever you\'d like to record on your screen, then go to <b class="ocpUI">Insert</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Screen recording</b>, and you\'ll be able to select a portion of your screen to record, capture what you need, and insert it directly into your presentation in one seamless process.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows insert screen recording button in PowerPoint2016" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/4a746bf5-baea-4511-a3cb-c9af7a6a97d9.png" title="Shows insert screen recording button in PowerPoint2016"/>\n</p>\n<p>\n<b class="ocpNote">\n<include item="note"></include> Note </b>\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0This feature is also available in PowerPoint 2013 if you have the most recent updates.</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Simpler sharing</h3>\n<p>Choose the <b class="ocpUI">Share</b> tab on the ribbon to share your presentation with others on SharePoint, OneDrive, or OneDrive for Business.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows the Share pane dialog in PowerPoint" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/f8667ce9-1a48-4bf4-b157-4f2b22f89f3e.png" title="Shows the Share pane dialog in PowerPoint"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Better conflict resolution</h3>\n<p>Now, when you\'re collaborating with others on a presentation and there are conflicts between changes you\'ve made and changes others have made, you\'ll see a side-by-side comparison of the slides with conflicting changes, so you can easily choose visually between which changes you\'d like to keep.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Shows conflicting changes dialog in PowerPoint" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/4c99bdbe-8eeb-4ee9-9e76-d5f99ff75cba.png" title="Shows conflicting changes dialog in PowerPoint"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Improved version history</h3>\n<p>Now you can go to <b class="ocpUI">File</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">History</b> to see a complete list of changes that have been made to your presentation and access earlier versions. </p>\n<p>\n<b class="ocpNote">\n<include item="note"></include> Note </b>\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0This feature is only supported for files stored on OneDrive for Business or SharePoint.</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Colorful, Dark Gray, and White Office  Themes</h3>\n<p>There are now four Office themes that you can apply to PowerPoint: Colorful, Dark Gray, Black, and White. To access these themes, go to <b class="ocpUI">File</b> &gt; <b class="ocpUI">Account</b>, and then click the drop down menu next to <b class="ocpUI">Office Theme</b>.</p>\n<p>\n<img alt="Office Theme pull down menu, Colorful, Dark Gray, and White theme options" src="https://osiprodeusodcspstoa01.blob.core.windows.net/en-us/media/d8b6780c-7b6a-4ad7-81a2-9d2d894a7d3b.png" title="Office Theme pull down menu, Colorful, Dark Gray, and White theme options"/>\n</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Better video resolution</h3>\n<p>Now, when you export your presentation as a video, you can choose to create a file with resolution as high as 1920 x 1080, which is great for presentations on larger screens.</p>\n<p>\n<b class="ocpNote">\n<include item="note"></include> Note </b>\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0This feature is also available in PowerPoint 2013 if you have the most recent updates.</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Improved Smart Guides</h3>\n<p>Smart Guides no longer turn off when you insert a table. Now you can use them to make sure everything, tables included, is aligned properly on your slides.</p>\n<p>\n<b class="ocpNote">\n<include item="note"></include> Note </b>\xc2\xa0\xc2\xa0This feature is also available in PowerPoint 2013 if you have the most recent updates.</p>\n</section>\n<section class="ocpSection">\n<h3>Quick Shape Formatting</h3>\n<p>This feature increases the number of default shape styles by introducing new \xe2\x80\x9cpreset\xe2\x80\x9d styles with theme colors to quickly get the look you want.</p>\n</section>\n</section>\n</article>\n</div>\n</div>\n<div
