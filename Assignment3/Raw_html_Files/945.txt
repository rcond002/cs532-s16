class="bgGradRelPos" id="bodycopy">\n<div class="contentpage currentpage" id="page-1">\n<p class="f-standfirst">\nFrom corporate responsibility to customer and employee engagement - The Peer Awards bangs the drum for good ideas. We take a look at some of the great things those shortlisted have been doing \n</p>\n<p>\nInspirational ideas are worth celebrating. Businesses all over the world are \ncoming up with innovative ways to improve community and boost customer and \nemployee engagement. <a href="http://thepeerawards.com">The Peer Awards</a> \ngives these ideas a platform, encouraging professionals from different \norganisations to celebrate the ideas of others, while sharing their own. \n</p><p>\nThe Peer Awards have shortlisted the most creative ideas submitted. These \nfinalists present their initiatives at the Peer Awards Conference on 18 \nJune, where everyone attending has their chance to vote, including the \nfinalists themselves. Winners will be announced at a sumptuous afternoon tea <a href="http://thepeerawards.com/the-awards-ceremony">Awards \nCeremony</a> at London\xe2\x80\x99s Waldorf Hilton on 25 September.\n</p><p>\n<preform><a href="http://thepeerawards.com" target="_blank"><img src="http://www.thetimes.co.uk/tto/multimedia/archive/00923/PeerHighRes_923407i.jpg"/></a></preform></p><p>\n<b>Here are some of the great things the finalists are up to</b>\n</p><p>\n<b>Corporate Responsibility (CSR)</b>\n</p><p>\nIn terms of CSR, there is a lot of great work being done by big and small \nbusiness alike. For example Sky is doing good works in a number of \ncategories. Sky Rainforest Rescue is helping save one billion trees in the \nAmazon and generating an increased awareness of deforestation, while Sky \nAcademy is using the power of TV, creativity and sport to help young people \nunlock their potential. \n</p><p>\nWorking with young people is a theme which occurs regularly. MetLife\xe2\x80\x99s \nLifeChanger initiative sends volunteers into schools to build children\xe2\x80\x99s \nunderstanding of basic financial concepts. Experian\xe2\x80\x99s Values, Money and Me \nprogramme helps primary schools in financially disadvantaged areas to be \ntransformed into Centres of Excellence in financial education. \n</p><p>\nMeanwhile, Cisco provides a week long work experience programme for year 10/11 \nstudents. With welcome and introduction to Cisco, group sessions, Dragons\xe2\x80\x99 \nDen style activity and soft skills development. IMPA ACT is establishing \nresponsible supply chain management across the shipping industry. \n</p><p>\n<b>Customer Engagement</b>\n</p><p>\nIncreasingly companies are recognising the importance to their business of \nputting the customer centre-stage. Brewing company Molson Coors\xe2\x80\x99 UK business \ncame up with Customers Inc., cardboard cutouts placed around the building \ndesigned to represent 13 customer types and remind employees to keep their \ncustomers in mind. \n</p><p>\nOver a thousand Tesco.com delivery drivers are, through working with team \nmotivation consultancy, NKD, becoming emotionally connected to delivering \ntheir best service to customers. \n</p><p>\n<b>Employee Engagement</b>\n</p><p>\nKeeping employees enthused and engaged is also becoming a priority for firms. \nThomson Reuters\xe2\x80\x99 Learning Passport is a campaign-based approach, using fun \nand competition to help with learning and development. Meanwhile, Global \nPayments\xe2\x80\x99 Living Our Culture was launched at a special event with the aim of \nimproving communication with integrity. \n</p><p>\nCisco\xe2\x80\x99s Be Amazing Programme has created activities around innovation, \nbuilding community, developing people, celebrating together, inspiring \npeople and generally making people proud to work at the company, because \npeople that are passionate about working for them and feel it\xe2\x80\x99s a great \nplace to work are good for Cisco. \n</p><p>\nMetLife\xe2\x80\x99s OneVoice features an innovative internal YouTube-style platform for \ncapturing and sharing employee-generated videos. All OMV\xe2\x80\x99s 25,500 employees \nhave the opportunity to get actively involved in implementing sustainability \nprojects. \n</p><p>\nEven non-customer-facing Molson Coors\xe2\x80\x99 employees are engaged with customers\xe2\x80\x99 \nneeds thanks to \xe2\x80\x98Customers Inc.\xe2\x80\x99. Placed across the UK business, 13 \nlife-size cardboard cut-out characters that represent every customer type \nand carry a message updated based on recent experience, are physical \nreminders to \xe2\x80\x98think customer\xe2\x80\x99. \n</p><p>\n<b>Nurturing Talent</b>\n</p><p>\nClever ways of nurturing talent can help companies to decrease staff turnover \nand find internal candidates for promotion to senior roles. Readers Offers \ndoes this by conveying how much individuals are valued as employees and \nLansons by championing women and focusing on female talent. \n</p><p>\nOthers find that a different approach to training is paying dividends. \nEversheds finds that a flexible approach to coaching achieves much more than \ntraining. The AA has adopted a strengths-based approach to attract and \ndevelop driving instructor talent and Carewatch finds that a collaborative \ntechnique is proving very effective, with managers becoming involved in \nmentoring. \n</p><p>\nFocus on British Gas \n</p><p>\nBritish Gas\xe2\x80\x99s <a href="http://thepeerawards.com/pa15-019-british-gas-community-action-partnership">Community \nAction Partnership</a> (CAP) is a \xc2\xa31.2m, two-year programme with fuel \npoverty charity National Energy Action. It tackles fuel poverty through a \ntailored partnership approach in eight localities across Britain, positively \naffecting 800,000 people. \n</p><p>\n<b>CAP works in three ways: </b>\n</p><p>\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Supporting local authorities to develop and implement their fuel poverty \nstrategies. It has worked with 18 local authorities and reached over 77,000 \npeople to date. \n</p><p>\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Training community workers to better identify signs of fuel poverty and \nprovide practical assistance to people struggling with bills. It has trained \nmore than 250 advisors so far \xe2\x80\x93 nearly 1,300 trained advisors will go on to \nassist over 37,000 people per year. \n</p><p>\n\xe2\x80\xa2 Engaging consumers in energy efficiency, money management and behaviour \nchange, helping them save money while keeping warm. Over 650 people to date \nhave learnt how to save energy and keep warm, through interactive games and \nquizzes at friendly community events. \n</p>\n</div>\n<div class="contentpage " id="page-2">\n<p>\nBritish Gas\xe2\x80\x99 community initiatives are not just about tackling fuel poverty. \nIn 2014, the British Gas <a href="http://thepeerawards.com/pa15-018-british-gas">Generation \nGreen</a> programme challenged children to think about energy creatively and \nreduce energy use in school. \n</p><p>\nPrizes worth up to \xc2\xa31m were available, with demonstrable environmental and \neducational benefits to the community.
